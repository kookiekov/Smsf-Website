How does a NPN transistor work?

A normal NPN transistor consists of three seperate legs: A Base(Which is labeled as 'b' in the diagram below), a Collector (Which is labeled as 'c' in the diagram below), and lastly an Emitter (Which is labeled as 'e' in the diagram below). So with that in mind if you allow a current to flow between the base and the emitter it will permit the flow of current from the c(Collector) towards the e(emitter).
